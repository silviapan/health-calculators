1) Am I at a healthy weight?

Body Mass Index

Body mass index (BMI) is a value that represents the relationship between your height and weight. While it cannot predict with absolute certainty the ideal weight and health of the individual, it can be a great indicator of body fat percentage and the associated health risks. For example, a high BMI is strongly correlated with a high level of excess body fat. Please fill out all fields to calculate your BMI.

2) How much should I eat?

Basal Metabolic Rate

All food contains calories, which are units of energy that your body utilizes to function. The basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the minimum amount of energy used by the body at rest, i.e. this is the amount of energy used only to keep your organs functioning. It varies according to your age, weight, height, sex, and body fat percentage.

Total Daily Energy Expenditure

The total daily energry expenditure (TDEE) is the total amount of energy needed in a day. It takes the BMR and multiplies it with a multiplier that reflects your daily activity level. Your TDEE is how much you would eat to maintain your current weight. One important theory to keep in mind is "Calories In, Calories Out". When calorie intake is greater than calorie expenditure, weight gain occurs. Conversely, when calorie intake is less than calorie expenditure, weight loss occurs.
